About Kolhapur Food : As the Kolhapur city is famous for its historical monuments and ancient temples it is also well known for special food dishes and snacks, for example, Kolhapuri Misal, Kolhapuri Bhel, and Non-veg food are some of the most popular food enjoyed in Kolhapur. It is considered that Kolhapuri food is very spicy food, but the truth is the food is not that spicy and nowadays hotels are keeping an option to serve mild food and snacks for the tourist. So in the following section, I have mentioned some top food joints in Kolhapur. If you are in Kolhapur or planning to visit then visit at least once to these food joints believe me you will love it.

Kolhapuri Missal Speciality :



A Missal is one of the topmost Kolhapur specialties. A Kolhapuri Missal is a delicious dish in terms of spicy and tasty. A kolhapuri missal is so adaptable that people eat it not only during breakfast but some people prefer to eat it during lunch and evening snacks. So people who like to taste and the spicy and tasty item they will definitely love Kolhapuri Missal. This dish is made up of sprouted Mutki (moth bean), Chivada, Shev, onion, potato, and on top of all they pour the spicy liquid. It is delicious and one of my favorites. Special Kolhapuri Non-Veg food :



One of the food items for which kolhapur is known in entire Maharashtra state is its non-vegetarian food. Non-Veg food is a very special and authentic. The non veg food especially like mutton and chicken is made using various authentic spices. If you are a non-vegetarian, then you should try one of these restaurants. There are lots of non-veg food hotels in Kolhapur but in the following section, I have mentioned the most visited and top-rated non-veg food joints. You should taste Tambada rassa (it is red meat soup) and Pandhara rassa (it is white meat soup), dry and fried mutton and mutton pickle. Kheema Balls Rice is also well known as a special dish as Golyachi Biryani. Tambada Rassa is a mutton soup generally hot & spicy, red in colour , while Pandhara Rassa is Mutton soup prepared by using white coconut milk and hot spices without chilly. Tambda & Pandhara Rassa are served along with the main course as accompaniments. Kolhapuri mutton masala & Mutton sukke (dry) are tender pieces of lamb meat cooked in a traditional way with special kolhapuri chutneys & masalas. There is also a trend to eat authentic non veg food at household eateries (called as khanavals) which are mainly served in thalis . If you are near Kolhapur central bus stand or Railway station, then nearest food joints are present near Ayodhya Park complex where you will find many restaurants.

Kolhapuri Chappal (footwear) :



Kolhapuri chappal are world famous for its complex and detail look. It is a major handicraft industry the employs over 20,000 crafts persons in the district. The Kolhapur chappals are flat and complicated and detail patterned handcrafted leather footwear traditionally made in kolhapur city and nearby places. Due to modernization these Kolhapuri chappal are also available in different shapes, patterns and colors. They are available for both men and women. You will find lots of footwear shops to buy these chappels.

Wrestling in Kolhapur :



In India, the biggest wrestling stadium is in Kolhapur in the state of Maharashtra and it is called as Kashbaug maidan (stadium) and it is heritage place as well. Khasbaug Maidan (full name- Rajarshi Shahu Khasbaug Maidan) – Khasbag Stadium is a national wrestling stadium in Kolhapur city. The stadium was built in the time of Rajarshi Shahu Maharaj and is almost a hundred year old stadium. Khasbaug Maidan (full name- Rajarshi Shahu Khasbaug Mai Khasbag stadium, also known as Khasbaug Maidan (Ground), is 1 km from Mahalaxmi Temple and 8 Km from Central Bus Stand of Kolhapur. The structure flaunts a seating arrangement for about 30,000 people around a Wrestling Ring. Special seating is available for the members of the royal family of Kolhapur on the east side of the wrestling ring. Wrestlers from Kolhapur who have won awards nationally and internationally Khasbha Jadhav India’s first Olympic medalist in individual category won the bronze medal in the year 1952 at Helsinki Games in wrestling. Dadu Chougule was awarded a Hind Kesari. Shreepati Khanchnale First Hind Kesari.

Kolhapuri Turban (also called pheta in Marathi) Kolhapuri Pheta (Turban) is a specialty in Kolhapur. Wearing a turban is considered as highly respectful way to treat people and is a customary honor to guest who comes from other place is welcomed by the offering him to wear the traditional Kolhapur turban. Actually the turban is a long cloth off 3.5 to 6 meters. It is the specific Saffron color is most choice for Kolhapuri turbans.

Kolhapuri Jaggery :



Kolhapur Jaggery is a sweet item which is concentrated product of sugarcane juice and the color vary from golden brown to dark brown. Kolhapuri Jaggery is very popular in Maharastra, so tourists mostly purchase the jaggery whenever they visit kolhapur. The Maharashtra state in India is the largest producer and consumer of jaggery.

Though there are various types of jewelries in Kolhapur city but one special type of Jewelry which is very popular and famous and is Kolhapuri saaj and it is nothing but a a special type of necklace. This piece of jewel is over 60 years now and has transformed into various patterns and designs over the past few decades as shown in below Kolhapuri saaj pictures. Kolhapuri saaj has special importance for Maharashtrian women. Now in modern life most of the people don’t know this information of Kolhapuri necklace. The kolhapuri saaj is made up of different designs of leaves and carvings in it. Small pendants having different designs are strung with a big ruby pendant. Nowadays due to cost factor instead of gold Kolhapuri saaj you can also get gold plated Kolhapuri saaj. You will find lot of shops in Gujri road Kolhapur.
